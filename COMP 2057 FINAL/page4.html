The Bite Incidents

The Bite of 87

This incident has been mentioned subtly throughout the series but there has not been any details that have been properly revealed. The identity of the animatronic that caused the attack and the specifics of the incident remain undisclosed, but the victim reportedly suffered a loss of their frontal lobe and yet managed to survive. This occurrence was among several other incidents that caused the company's reputation to diminish, resulting in a significant decrease in business in the future.
Following the incident, the animatronics were restricted from roaming around the building during the day, and the management opted to limit their free movement to the nighttime to avoid their servos from locking up, as explained by Phone Guy.
This incident has commonly been mixed up with the bite of 83.


The Bite of 83

During the cutscene after completing Night 5 in Five Nights at Freddy's 4, fans were introduced to a similar incident known as "The Bite of '83". The game's events occurred in 1983 and revolved around a child who was being bullied by older kids and his older brother Michael during his party at Fredbear's Family Diner. The bullies forced the victim towards Fredbear's stage and had Fredbear "give the child a kiss" by pushing him into its mouth. The protagonist's head became stuck in the animatronic's mouth, which then crunched down on the victim's head, whether by accident or not.
Ultimately, we are led to believe that the indirect murdering of his own younger brother, led Micheal Afton to dig deeper into his fathers animatronics, making him the character we play as when working the night shift in the games.
